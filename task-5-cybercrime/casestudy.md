## Cybercrime Case Study – Phishing

Phishing is a type of cybercrime where attackers try to steal personal information such as passwords, bank details, or OTPs by pretending to be a trusted source.

In a typical phishing attack, a user receives an email or message that looks like it is from a bank or a well-known company. The message asks the user to click on a link and enter their personal details. Once the user enters the information, it is collected by the attacker.

Students and working professionals are often targeted because they frequently use emails and online services. The consequences can include loss of money, identity theft, and misuse of personal data.

This type of cybercrime is increasing rapidly due to the growing use of digital platforms.
